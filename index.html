About

I study the economics of coastal adaptation in a world of rising seas — how to weigh costs against benefits, finding the economically robust choice.

As a doctoral researcher at the Global Climate Forum, my work brings together economic modeling, applied econometrics, and environmental valuation inside the DIVA framework — a state-of-the-art global coastal impact and adaptation model.

Research interests

  • Coastal adaptation under sea-level rise — gray, green, and hybrid strategies
  • Environmental valuation of coastal & marine ecosystem services
  • Cost–benefit analysis at global and regional scales
  • Integrated economic–biophysical modeling (DIVA, CGE, ecosystem production functions)
  • Climate risk & resilience for built environment and infrastructure
